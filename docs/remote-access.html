Remote Access

Squid is most useful when your local machine keeps working while you're away from the desk — couch coding from a phone or tablet.

Tailscale

Tailscale's Personal plan is free for non-commercial use and creates a private WireGuard network across your own devices. Your phone, tablet, laptop, and workstation can talk to each other inside that private network without opening a public port.

Squid always binds to 127.0.0.1 — it never exposes itself directly on the network. bin/start.sh configures Tailscale's HTTPS proxy automatically if Tailscale is installed:

bin/start.sh   # configures tailscale serve automatically

Connecting from your phone

Type /remote in the chat to get a QR code with the full HTTPS URL and your access token already included — point your phone camera at it to open Squid in one tap:

https://<machine-name>.<tailnet>.ts.net/?token=<your-token>

The full domain is required. The short hostname alone (https://<machine-name>/) won't work, because Tailscale's TLS certificate is scoped to *.ts.net and browsers enforce an exact match.
